# Fertilizer Reference: 20 Types (Nutrient %, Form, Use Notes)

## Notes for simulator use

- Percentages are typical analysis values; products vary by manufacturer.
- Forms are listed to support availability and loss mechanics (for example volatilization, leaching, fixation).

## 1) Urea

- Analysis: 46% N
- Main nutrient form: Amide-N (converted to ammonium then nitrate)
- Typical use: Broadacre N source, often top-dressed.
- Mechanics hint: Surface-applied urea has volatilization risk without rain/incorporation.

## 2) Ammonium sulfate

- Analysis: 21% N, 24% S
- Main nutrient form: Ammonium-N, sulfate-S
- Typical use: N plus readily available sulfur.
- Mechanics hint: Good for S-deficient sands; lower volatilization than urea.

## 3) Calcium ammonium nitrate (CAN)

- Analysis: 27% N
- Main nutrient form: Ammonium + nitrate-N
- Typical use: In-crop N with lower volatilization sensitivity.
- Mechanics hint: Faster early N response than pure amide sources.

## 4) UAN solution

- Analysis: 32% N (varies 28 to 32)
- Main nutrient form: Urea + ammonium + nitrate
- Typical use: Liquid N delivery, often streamed.
- Mechanics hint: Allows split N strategy with operational flexibility.

## 5) Anhydrous ammonia

- Analysis: 82% N
- Main nutrient form: Ammonia (rapidly ammonium in soil)
- Typical use: Injected pre-plant N.
- Mechanics hint: Very high concentration; requires injection, low direct surface loss if sealed.

## 6) MAP (monoammonium phosphate)

- Analysis: 11% N, 22% P (52% P2O5)
- Main nutrient form: Ammonium-N, water-soluble phosphate
- Typical use: Starter P at seeding.
- Mechanics hint: High P efficiency when banded near seed at safe rates.

## 7) DAP (diammonium phosphate)

- Analysis: 18% N, 20% P (46% P2O5)
- Main nutrient form: Ammonium-N, phosphate
- Typical use: Starter N plus P.
- Mechanics hint: Alkaline reaction around granule can affect seed safety at high rates.

## 8) Triple superphosphate (TSP)

- Analysis: 20% P (46% P2O5)
- Main nutrient form: Water-soluble phosphate
- Typical use: High-analysis P source without N.
- Mechanics hint: Useful where P is needed but N timing is separate.

## 9) Single superphosphate (SSP)

- Analysis: 8 to 9% P (16 to 20% P2O5), 10 to 12% S
- Main nutrient form: Phosphate + sulfate-S
- Typical use: P and S supply, especially in pastures and sandy soils.
- Mechanics hint: Lower P concentration but valuable S co-supply.

## 10) Muriate of potash (MOP, potassium chloride)

- Analysis: 50% K (60% K2O)
- Main nutrient form: Potassium + chloride
- Typical use: Primary K fertilizer.
- Mechanics hint: Strongly lifts soil K; chloride-sensitive crops may need caution.

## 11) Sulfate of potash (SOP, potassium sulfate)

- Analysis: 41% K (50% K2O), 17 to 18% S
- Main nutrient form: Potassium + sulfate-S
- Typical use: K supply where chloride load should be low.
- Mechanics hint: Better fit for chloride-sensitive systems and S support.

## 12) Potassium nitrate

- Analysis: 13% N, 38% K (46% K2O)
- Main nutrient form: Nitrate-N + potassium
- Typical use: High-value crops, fertigation.
- Mechanics hint: Rapidly available N and K, usually premium cost.

## 13) Gypsum

- Analysis: 22 to 23% Ca, 17 to 18% S
- Main nutrient form: Calcium sulfate
- Typical use: Calcium and sulfur source; subsoil amelioration support.
- Mechanics hint: Supplies Ca without raising pH much.

## 14) Lime (aglime)

- Analysis: 30 to 40% Ca (as carbonate equivalent)
- Main nutrient form: Calcium carbonate
- Typical use: Raise pH and reduce exchangeable Al toxicity.
- Mechanics hint: Slow pH correction, strong long-term effect in acidic soils.

## 15) Dolomite

- Analysis: 18 to 22% Ca, 10 to 13% Mg (carbonate form)
- Main nutrient form: Calcium-magnesium carbonate
- Typical use: Lime source where Mg is also deficient.
- Mechanics hint: Raises pH while supplying Mg.

## 16) Epsom salt (magnesium sulfate)

- Analysis: 9 to 10% Mg, 13% S
- Main nutrient form: Magnesium sulfate
- Typical use: Fast Mg and S correction.
- Mechanics hint: Highly soluble; useful for quick response.

## 17) Ammonium thiosulfate (ATS)

- Analysis: 12% N, 26% S
- Main nutrient form: Ammonium-N + thiosulfate-S (oxidizes to sulfate)
- Typical use: Liquid N-S blends.
- Mechanics hint: S release delayed relative to sulfate forms.

## 18) Zinc sulfate

- Analysis: 21 to 36% Zn (form dependent)
- Main nutrient form: Sulfate-bound Zn, generally soluble
- Typical use: Correct Zn deficiency in cereals and oilseeds.
- Mechanics hint: High pH soils have greater Zn deficiency risk.

## 19) Borax / sodium borate

- Analysis: about 11% B
- Main nutrient form: Borate
- Typical use: Correct boron deficiency in canola and legumes.
- Mechanics hint: Very narrow safe range; toxicity risk if over-applied.

## 20) Sodium molybdate

- Analysis: about 39% Mo
- Main nutrient form: Molybdate
- Typical use: Seed or foliar trace supply for legumes and acidic soils.
- Mechanics hint: Small rates can strongly improve nodulation and N fixation.

## Optional mechanic attributes to store per fertilizer

- Solubility class: fast, medium, slow
- Loss risk: volatilization, leaching, denitrification
- pH effect: acidifying, neutral, liming
- Salt index: low, medium, high
- Seed safety index: safe, caution, avoid high in-furrow rates
