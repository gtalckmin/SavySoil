# Soil Savvy - Student Advisor Game

## Overview

**Soil Savvy** is an interactive educational game that teaches students about soil fertility analysis, nutrient management, and fertilizer recommendations for crop production. Students act as agricultural advisors, analyzing soil test results and building tailored fertilizer plans to optimize crop yields while managing costs.

## What Is This Game For?

This game helps students develop practical skills in:
- **Soil Analysis**: Understanding soil nutrient levels and identifying deficiencies
- **Crop Nutrition**: Learning specific nutrient requirements for different crops
- **Fertilizer Selection**: Choosing appropriate fertilizers based on nutrient composition
- **Application Timing**: Deciding when to apply nutrients during crop growth stages (sowing, emergence, vegetative)
- **Precision Farming**: Balancing yield optimization with cost efficiency
- **Decision-Making**: Making evidence-based agricultural recommendations

## How to Play

### 1. **Select Your Scenario**
- Choose a **crop** (Wheat, Canola, or Lupins)
- Choose a **soil type** (one of 10 distinct soil profiles)
- Click **"New Scenario"** to load the soil analysis

### 2. **Read the Soil Analysis Report**
You'll see:
- Soil test results for 8 nutrients (N, P, K, S, Ca, Zn, B, Mo)
- Status indicators showing which nutrients are deficient, adequate, or excess
- A radar chart comparing current soil levels to crop nutrient targets
- Soil properties (pH, organic matter, cation exchange capacity)

### 3. **Build Your Fertilizer Recommendation**
Using the Fertilizer Builder, you'll recommend:
- **Which fertilizers** to apply (20+ realistic options including nitrogen, phosphorus, potassium, sulfur, calcium, lime, and micronutrients)
- **Application rates** (in kg/ha)
- **Application timing** (Sowing, Emergence, or Vegetative stages)

The radar chart updates in real-time to show how your additions would affect final soil nutrient levels.

### 4. **Check Your Score**
Click **"Check Score"** to receive feedback on your recommendation:
- **Yield Score** (0-100): How well your plan meets crop nutrient requirements
- **Cost Score** (0-100): How efficiently you managed fertilizer expenses
- **Total Score**: Overall recommendation quality
- **Detailed Feedback**: Specific comments on your nutrient choices and application timing

## Main Rules

### Nutrient Targets
Each crop has specific target ranges for 8 nutrients:
- **Macronutrients**: Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), Potassium (K), Sulfur (S), Calcium (Ca)
- **Micronutrients**: Zinc (Zn), Boron (B), Molybdenum (Mo)

Nutrients must fall within crop targets to achieve good yield scores.

### Soil Status Indicators
- 🟢 **Adequate**: Nutrient is in target range → No major action needed
- 🟡 **Deficient**: Nutrient below target → Must be supplemented with fertilizer
- 🔴 **Excess**: Nutrient above target → Adding more could cause problems

### Application Timing
Certain nutrients benefit from strategic timing:
- **Sowing**: Phosphorus and potassium are often applied at planting
- **Emergence**: Early supplements support seedling vigor
- **Vegetative**: Nitrogen is often split across growth stages

Correct timing earns bonus points.

### Cost Efficiency
Your total fertilizer cost affects your score:
- **< 60 $/ha**: Maximum cost score (100)
- **60-120 $/ha**: Excellent efficiency (95)
- **120-180 $/ha**: Good efficiency (85)
- **180-250 $/ha**: Acceptable efficiency (70)
- **> 250 $/ha**: High cost - yield score may not justify expense

### Crop-Specific Considerations

**Wheat**
- Requires strong nitrogen supply (minimum 45 kg/ha)
- Benefits from phosphorus support
- Important for market-grade production

**Canola**
- Particularly responsive to sulfur (minimum 12 kg/ha)
- High nitrogen needs (60-120 kg/ha)
- Boron requirements are critical

**Lupins**
- Nitrogen-fixing legume (excess fertilizer N can suppress nodulation)
- Strong phosphorus requirements
- Benefits from potassium and molybdenum

## Scoring System

### Yield Score (Primary Component)
- Starts at 100 points
- Penalizes still-deficient nutrients (-15 points each)
- Penalizes nutrient excesses (-10 points each)
- Rewards strategic nutrient additions and correct timing (+3-5 points each)
- Final: **0-100 based on crop nutrition quality**

### Cost Score (Secondary Component)
- Emphasizes efficiency without compromising yield
- Rewards frugal, well-targeted recommendations
- Final: **0-100 based on fertilizer expenditure**

### Recommendation Quality
- **80-100**: Excellent - Optimal nutrient balance and efficiency
- **60-79**: Good - Adequate nutrition, reasonable cost
- **40-59**: Fair - Missing some nutrients or over-fertilized
- **20-39**: Poor - Significant gaps or wasteful spending
- **0-19**: Ineffective - No recommendation or major problems

## Tips for Success

1. **Start with deficiencies** - Always address nutrients marked as deficient first
2. **Consider timing** - Phosphorus at sowing, nitrogen split across stages
3. **Match crop needs** - Focus on each crop's critical nutrients (e.g., sulfur for canola)
4. **Avoid excess** - Over-fertilizing can reduce scores and waste money
5. **Use multi-purpose products** - Products like ammonium sulfate supply both N and S efficiently
6. **Strategic timing matters** - Placing P at sowing boosts early vigour
7. **Check the radar** - The chart shows if you're hitting nutrient targets in real time
