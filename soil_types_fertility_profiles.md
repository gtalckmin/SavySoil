# Ten Distinct Soil Types With Reasonable Fertility Profiles

## Notes

- Values are indicative starting points for a simulation, not site-specific recommendations.
- Units:
  - Organic carbon (OC): percent
  - Colwell P, exchangeable K, sulfate-S, Zn: mg/kg
  - CEC: cmol(+)/kg
  - pH: CaCl2 scale
  - Exchangeable Al: percent of CEC

## Soil profiles

### 1) Deep yellow sand (low rainfall sandplain)

- Texture: Sand
- OC: 0.7
- CEC: 3.5
- pH (CaCl2): 5.6
- Colwell P: 14
- Exchangeable K: 42
- Sulfate-S: 5
- Zn: 0.5
- Exchangeable Al: 1
- Fertility character: Low nutrient holding capacity, leaching-prone, frequent N/S/K constraints.

### 2) Pale deep sand (water repellent topsoil risk)

- Texture: Coarse sand
- OC: 0.5
- CEC: 2.8
- pH (CaCl2): 5.2
- Colwell P: 10
- Exchangeable K: 30
- Sulfate-S: 4
- Zn: 0.4
- Exchangeable Al: 3
- Fertility character: Very low buffering, low nutrient reserves, acidity and repellence can reduce establishment.

### 3) Sandy loam duplex (sand over clay)

- Texture: Sandy loam topsoil over clay subsoil
- OC: 1.1
- CEC: 7.5
- pH (CaCl2): 5.4
- Colwell P: 18
- Exchangeable K: 95
- Sulfate-S: 8
- Zn: 0.8
- Exchangeable Al: 4
- Fertility character: Moderate topsoil fertility, subsoil constraints possible, responsive to balanced nutrition.

### 4) Loam (well-structured cropping loam)

- Texture: Loam
- OC: 1.6
- CEC: 11.5
- pH (CaCl2): 5.9
- Colwell P: 24
- Exchangeable K: 135
- Sulfate-S: 11
- Zn: 1.1
- Exchangeable Al: 1
- Fertility character: Good general fertility and water holding, broad nutrient responsiveness.

### 5) Clay loam (higher buffering, moderate sodicity risk)

- Texture: Clay loam
- OC: 1.8
- CEC: 16.0
- pH (CaCl2): 6.2
- Colwell P: 20
- Exchangeable K: 180
- Sulfate-S: 10
- Zn: 1.0
- Exchangeable Al: 0
- Fertility character: Strong nutrient buffering, often P fixation and structural constraints if sodic.

### 6) Cracking clay vertosol

- Texture: Heavy clay
- OC: 2.1
- CEC: 28.0
- pH (CaCl2): 7.1
- Colwell P: 16
- Exchangeable K: 260
- Sulfate-S: 9
- Zn: 0.7
- Exchangeable Al: 0
- Fertility character: High K and CEC, but P/Zn availability may limit early growth.

### 7) Red-brown earth (neutral to slightly alkaline)

- Texture: Clay loam to light clay
- OC: 1.3
- CEC: 13.5
- pH (CaCl2): 6.8
- Colwell P: 19
- Exchangeable K: 150
- Sulfate-S: 9
- Zn: 0.6
- Exchangeable Al: 0
- Fertility character: Generally productive, micronutrient availability can decline as pH rises.

### 8) Calcareous loam (free lime present)

- Texture: Loam with carbonate
- OC: 1.0
- CEC: 10.0
- pH (CaCl2): 7.8
- Colwell P: 12
- Exchangeable K: 120
- Sulfate-S: 7
- Zn: 0.4
- Exchangeable Al: 0
- Fertility character: Al toxicity absent, but P, Zn, Mn availability often constrained at high pH.

### 9) Acid gravelly loam (upland duplex/gravel)

- Texture: Gravelly loam
- OC: 1.2
- CEC: 6.8
- pH (CaCl2): 4.7
- Colwell P: 15
- Exchangeable K: 85
- Sulfate-S: 7
- Zn: 0.9
- Exchangeable Al: 9
- Fertility character: Strong acidity and Al toxicity risk, liming often high priority.

### 10) Peaty sandy loam (high OC, low bulk density)

- Texture: Organic-rich sandy loam
- OC: 4.5
- CEC: 18.5
- pH (CaCl2): 5.0
- Colwell P: 22
- Exchangeable K: 110
- Sulfate-S: 13
- Zn: 0.8
- Exchangeable Al: 2
- Fertility character: Good sulfur and N mineralization potential, but Cu/Zn and pH management can be critical.

## Suggested simulation tags per soil

- Low-buffering: 1, 2
- Acidity-sensitive: 2, 3, 9, 10
- High pH micronutrient risk: 7, 8
- High CEC/high K: 6
- Broadly fertile baseline: 4, 5
